Source: https://www.fau.edu/registrar/university-catalog/catalog/degree-req/

================================================================================
ASSOCIATE IN ARTS (A.A.) DEGREE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

To earn an A.A. degree from FAU, students must:

1. Earn a minimum of 60 credits with at least a cumulative 2.0 FAU GPA
2. Earn a minimum of 40 of the 60 credits at lower-division (1000-2000) level
3. Earn a minimum of 30 of the 40 lower-division credits at FAU
   (may be waived for students transferring 30+ lower-division credits from
   a single Florida public state college)
4. Earn a minimum of 30 of the 60 credits in residence at FAU; complete the
   last semester in residence at FAU
5. Apply no more than 30 credits of non-traditional credit (AP, CLEP,
   Correspondence, IB, Military Service Schools)
6. Fulfill General Education Curriculum requirements
7. Satisfy Writing Across Curriculum and College-Level Computation Skills requirements
8. Meet the Foreign Language Entrance Requirement (FLENT)
9. Complete the Florida Civic Literacy Requirement (for students entering fall 2018+)
10. Submit an Associate in Arts degree application to University Advising Services

Students may not receive an A.A. degree from both FAU and a home FCS institution.

Two application options:
1. A.A. degree awarded at FAU
2. A.A. degree awarded to FCS transfer students by the home FCS institution through
   reverse transfer of credits earned at FAU

Note: After completing A.A. requirements, students may apply in any semester prior
to the semester their baccalaureate degree is awarded. If the baccalaureate is not
completed, the A.A. must be requested within 5 years. Students with an "I" (incomplete)
or "NR" (no reported grade) on their transcripts may not apply until those grades
are resolved.

================================================================================
BACCALAUREATE DEGREE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

To earn a baccalaureate degree, students must:

1. Earn a minimum of 120 credits with a minimum 2.0 GPA for all FAU coursework
2. Earn a minimum of 42 of 120 credits at upper division (3000-level and above)
3. Apply no more than 60 non-traditional credits (Credit by Exam, Correspondence,
   Military Service Schools)
4. Earn the last 25% of total required credits at FAU
5. Earn at least 75% of all upper-division credits required for the major from FAU
   (some majors may require more — consult degree requirements for your major)
6. Fulfill General Education Curriculum and Civic Literacy requirements
7. Summer Credit Requirement: Earn a minimum of 9 credits in one or more summer terms
   at FAU or another Florida State University System institution
   (applies only to students admitted as freshmen or transfer students with fewer than
   60 credits; per Florida Board of Governors Regulation 6.016)
8. Satisfy College-Level Communication and Computation Skills requirements
   (formerly Gordon Rule)
9. Fulfill lower-division college and department requirements
10. Fulfill the foreign language graduation requirement (all B.S. and B.A. programs
    unless otherwise specified)
11. Submit an Application for Degree form by the end of the third week of the
    semester in which the student expects to graduate

Academic Learning Compacts:
Each baccalaureate program identifies (1) content/discipline knowledge and skills,
(2) communication skills, and (3) critical thinking skills students must demonstrate
before graduation. Print copies available by contacting the appropriate department.

================================================================================
SECOND BACCALAUREATE DEGREE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

To earn a second baccalaureate degree, students must:

1. Earn a minimum of 30 credits in residence at FAU beyond those required for the
   first degree. Students earning two degrees simultaneously (dual degree) must earn
   at least 150 credits total.
2. Earn at least 75% of all upper-division credits required for the major from FAU
3. Satisfy admission and graduation requirements of the granting department and college.
   Students with a bachelor's from an accredited four-year institution are deemed to
   have met FAU's general education requirements. Foreign language still required if
   applicable to one of the two degrees.
4. Submit an Application for Degree form

================================================================================
MINOR REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

- Students may declare no more than two minors
- Must earn at least 75% of all credits required for the minor from FAU
  (some departments may require more)
- A minor can only be pursued if its requirements can be completed without extending
  the anticipated graduation date (see Timely Graduation Policy)

================================================================================
COMBINED PROGRAMS (BACHELOR'S + GRADUATE)
================================================================================

Combined bachelor's/master's degree:
  - Minimum 150 credits total
  - 120 credits minimum for baccalaureate
  - 30 credits minimum for master's
  - All master's credits must be at graduate level (5000+)
  - Maximum 12 graduate credits may count toward both degrees
  - Baccalaureate degree conferred before graduate degree

Combined bachelor's/doctoral degree:
  - Minimum 192 credits total
  - 120 credits minimum for baccalaureate
  - 72 credits minimum for doctoral

Individual programs may have more stringent requirements.

================================================================================
APPLICATION FOR DEGREE
================================================================================

ASSOCIATE IN ARTS:
  Apply by the end of the third week of the semester of expected graduation.
  Cannot apply for A.A. and baccalaureate in the same semester.
  A.A. recipients do not participate in commencement.
  Contact: University Advising Services, 561-297-3064

BACCALAUREATE:
  Apply using the Application for Degree form by the end of the third week of the
  semester of expected graduation. It is the student's responsibility to meet all
  graduation requirements. Refer to the Academic Calendar for specific dates.

GRADUATING STUDENT SURVEY:
  Required for all graduating students. Link emailed two weeks before last day of
  classes. Must be completed to obtain official transcript.
