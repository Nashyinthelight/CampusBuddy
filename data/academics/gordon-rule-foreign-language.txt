================================================================================
FOUNDATIONAL COURSE REQUIREMENT
================================================================================

All students (freshmen and transfer) must complete the following within the
prescribed timeframes after matriculation at FAU:

  ENC 1101 (College Writing 1)                  — Complete within first year
  General Education Mathematics (at least 1)    — Complete within first year
  Foreign Language Requirement                  — Complete within two years
  Civic Literacy Requirement                    — Complete within two years

Students should check their Degree Audit Report to confirm which requirements
have already been met before enrollment.

================================================================================
COLLEGE-LEVEL COMMUNICATION AND COMPUTATION SKILLS (FORMERLY GORDON RULE)
================================================================================

FAU complies with the State Board of Education requirement for College-Level
Communication and Computation Skills. This requires students to complete with
grades of "C" or higher:
  - 12 credits of writing
  - 6 credits of mathematics (at or above the level of college algebra)

Writing breakdown:
  - 6 credits of English coursework: ENC 1101 + ENC 1102 (or approved substitute)
  - 6 credits of additional coursework requiring college-level writing through
    multiple assignments

Computation: Typically satisfied through the GEC Mathematics requirement.

Out-of-state transfer students who believe they have completed equivalent courses
with "C" or better should submit a course evaluation form with either a course
syllabus or an official letter from the previous institution to:
  Email: treval@fau.edu
  OR in person at the Registrar's Office, Boca Raton campus

FOREIGN LANGUAGE REQUIREMENT
================================================================================

FAU has two distinct language requirements:

FLENT — Foreign Language Entrance Requirement:
  State University System admission requirement. Requires two years of the same
  language in high school. FAU may waive FLENT for admission purposes. However,
  if FLENT is waived, the student must satisfy FLEX regardless of degree pursued.

FLEX — Foreign Language Exit/Graduation Requirement:
  Two semesters of the same foreign language at the first-year college level,
  or demonstrated equivalent proficiency.

  Required for:
    - All B.S. degree programs (unless otherwise specified)
    - All B.A. degree programs
    - Select other degree programs
    - Any student whose FLENT was waived

FLEX may be satisfied by any of the following:
  1. Successful completion of two semesters of a first-year college-level sequence
     (XXX 1120 and XXX 1121) of the same foreign language
  2. Successful completion of the second semester of a first-year sequence (XXX 1121)
     OR a higher-level foreign language course
  3. Placement exam with passing score — CLEP, AP, or IB
     (see Acceleration Mechanisms section of catalog for required passing scores)
  4. Significant prior exposure without a CLEP/AP/IB exam: contact the Department
     of Languages, Linguistics and Comparative Literature for a standardized exam
  5. Secondary or post-secondary curriculum taught in a foreign language: the
     Department of Languages, Linguistics and Comparative Literature can evaluate
     official school records to determine if proficiency has been met
  6. Successful completion of the second semester of a first-year American Sign
     Language sequence (unless specified differently by the degree program)

Standard FAU course numbering:
  First-year, first-semester = 1120 (e.g., SPN 1120)
  First-year, second-semester = 1121 (e.g., SPN 1121)

Placement guideline:
  1 year of high school foreign language = 1 semester of college foreign language.
  Example: 1 year of high school Spanish → enroll in SPN 1121.
  Note: If more than 3 years have elapsed since high school language study,
  this guideline may not apply.

================================================================================
CIVIC LITERACY REQUIREMENT
================================================================================

Pursuant to Florida Statute 8.006, baccalaureate degree-seeking students initially
entering a Florida college system (FCS) or state university system (SUS) in fall 2018
and thereafter must demonstrate competency in civic literacy. Students are expected
to complete this requirement by the end of their first year at FAU. No student may
graduate without having met this requirement.

COHORT 1: Students entering SUS or FCS before fall 2018
  — Civic Literacy Requirement does NOT apply.

COHORT 2: Students entering from fall 2018 to summer (terms 1 and 2) 2021
  — Must complete a course OR an exam.

COHORT 3: Students entering in summer (term 3) 2021 and thereafter
  — Must complete BOTH a course AND an exam.
  Exception: High school students who passed the Florida Civic Literacy Examination
  starting fall 2021 are exempt from the postsecondary exam but still must complete
  the course. Certain accelerated mechanisms also meet the course requirement.

OPTIONS FOR COHORTS 2 AND 3:

Courses (meet Course Competency):
  AMH 2010 — United States History to 1877
    (fall 2024 and after, including dual enrollment fall 2024 and after)
    Passing grade → Meets Course Competency
  AMH 2020 — United States History since 1877
    (taken after fall 2018, including dual enrollment after fall 2018)
    Passing grade → Meets Course Competency
  POS 2041 — Government of the United States
    (taken after fall 2018, including dual enrollment after fall 2018)
    Passing grade → Meets Course Competency

Accelerated Mechanisms:
  AP Government and Politics: United States Exam
    Score of 3 → Meets Course AND Exam Competency
  AP United States History Exam
    Score of 4 → Meets Course AND Exam Competency
  Cambridge AICE: History, U.S. History, c. 1840-1990 (A-Level) Exam
    Grade A-E → Meets Course Competency only
  CLEP: American Government Exam
    Score of 50 → Meets Course AND Exam Competency
  IB History, History of America Exam
    Score 5-7 → Meets Course Competency only

Additional Assessments (meet Exam Competency only):
  U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services Naturalization Test - Civics
    Score of 60 out of 100
  Florida Civic Literacy Examination (FLCE) (available fall 2021 and after)
    Score of 48 out of 80

Testing options: remotely via Honorlock OR at the FAU Testing and Certification Center.
Nominal fees apply.
Free Civic Literacy workshops are available to help students prepare.
Contact: https://www.fau.edu/ugstudies/civic-literacy-requirement/index.php
