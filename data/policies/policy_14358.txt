TITLE: Healthcare Billing Compliance Policy

University Policy
Office of the President
University Policy
7.4 Healthcare Billing Compliance Policy
Former Policy Number: 8.4
APPLICABILITY:
This policy is applicable to all Covered Components of the University that are engaged in billing for healthcare services.
POLICY STATEMENT:
Florida Atlantic University (“FAU”) has a strong commitment to ensuring that the billing occurring in connection with its healthcare services are conducted in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. A critical component of any billing compliance policy for academic health providers is professional fee reimbursement. The regulatory requirements governing such reimbursements are complex and changing. To underscore and enhance its commitment, and to better assist all employees, including faculty physicians and practitioners in this area, the University is implementing this policy.
RESPONSBILITIES:
1. Office of Compliance and Ethics
a. The Chief Compliance and Ethics Officer, in collaboration with the Billing Compliance Committee (“BCC”), develops University-wide standards for billing compliance.  A representative from the Office of Compliance and Ethics chairs the BCC.
2. Covered Components
a. The Covered Components are responsible for developing and implementing their respective billing compliance plans. Each Covered Component must designate a representative to assist with the implementation of its Billing Compliance Plan that will serve on the BCC.
3. Office of General Counsel (OGC)
a. Advises the BCC on legal and regulatory requirements regarding healthcare billing and provides legal assistance during investigations of suspected violations reported.
4. Division of Financial Affairs
a. Provides financial oversight to ensure billing practices align with University’s accounting standards for accuracy and compliance.
5.  BCC
a. Provides oversight of the University’s Covered Components healthcare billing operations.
b. Comprised of the following members: representatives of each Covered Component with expertise in their respective Covered Component’s billing practices, a representative of the OGC, a representative from the Division of Financial Affairs, and chaired by a representative from the Office of Compliance and Ethics. The BCC must:
(1) Review and implement University policies to guide compliance with healthcare billing operations;
(2) Review and approve each component’s billing compliance plans and policies;
(3) Support healthcare billing training and education initiatives;
(4) Review and approve the audit methodology adopted by each Covered Component’s Billing Compliance Plan; and
(5) Develop, with the assistance of University counsel, appropriate corrective action plans to address billing compliance issues that have been identified.
PROCEDURES:
Billing Compliance Plan
There shall be a University-wide billing compliance plan developed and reviewed annually every fiscal year. Each Covered Component must prepare an annual billing compliance plan to implement its respective billing compliance initiatives. A summary of the essential elements of a Billing Compliance Plan are detailed in this policy. Before becoming effective, such plans must be reviewed and approved by the BCC to ensure consistency with overall policies. Each Covered Component shall have the authority to direct specific billing practices designed to enhance compliance, including, but not limited to, (1) the use of particular codes for designated services, (2) the procedures and practices used to handle billing, or (3) the imposition of restrictions on billing by particular physicians, or groups of physicians, or other health professionals. The unit-specific healthcare billing compliance plan must have the following key features:
a. Healthcare billing-related training and education concerning applicable legal requirements and University policies;
b. Regular chart and billing reviews by University employees and their designees to assess compliance and to identify potential issues;
c. A mechanism for conducting regular audits of each Covered Component’s healthcare operations;
(1) The BCC may initiate its own audit at the discretion of the Chief Compliance and Ethics Officer.
d. Processes for individuals to raise healthcare billing questions and receive appropriate guidance concerning healthcare billing; and,
e. Address any instances of non-compliance with University or Covered Component healthcare billing requirements or policies. Upon identification of a billing compliance issue, the Covered Component shall develop and implement a corrective action plan to address the matter, mitigate recurrence, and communicate findings to the appropriate supervisory authority.
