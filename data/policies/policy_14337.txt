University Policy
6.2 Visiting Dignitaries
Former Policy Number: 9.3
APPLICABILITY:
This policy applies to all University departments, units, organizations, and affiliated entities — including recognized student organizations and student-run programming — that host a visiting dignitary in person or virtually.
POLICY STATEMENT:
Over the years, Florida Atlantic University (FAU) has hosted many national and international dignitaries at a variety of university and community events. The visit of a dignitary to the University can present an excellent opportunity for FAU students, faculty, staff and the community to engage in intellectual exchange with an important local, state, national or international figure. A visit may also demand a heightened sensitivity to security and/or protocol issues: attract a large audience; require coordination with state and federal officials and agencies; and require intense logistical coordination.  Simply, visits by certain dignitaries may require the commitment of FAU resources beyond the authority of the person, unit, or entity arranging the visit. Moreover, FAU has an institutional interest in ensuring that visits by dignitaries are successful, both in terms of how the dignitary is treated, as well as in achieving FAU’s interest in ensuring that visit provides the broadest possible benefit to the FAU Community. This policy establishes the responsibilities and procedure for obtaining approval of and arranging dignitary visits to ensure that these visits are well-coordinated throughout the University, and that FAU will be well-remembered by our distinguished visitors. The purpose of these protocols is to: (i) ensure that the University adheres to a consistent set of procedures in relation to engagement with visiting dignitaries; (ii) present FAU as a coordinated, professional and respectful institution; (iii) help develop, maintain and enhance important relationships with highly influential individuals in public office or positions of influence; and (iv) ensure the protocols of official positions are adhered to by the University.

(1) United States Presidents, Vice Presidents or spouses
(2) United States Members of Congress (senators and representatives) or spouses
(3) United States Cabinet Members or spouses
b. State of Florida Officials – examples include but are not limited to:
(1) Governor, Lt. Governor, and spouses
(2) Florida cabinet members
(3) State Senators or Representatives
(4) Chancellor, SUS System
(5) Board of Governors, SUS System
(6) University President
(7) Any other elected or appointed state official
c. FAU Board of Trustees
d. Local Elected or Appointed officials, including but not limited to:
(1) Mayors
(2) County commissioners
(3) City council members
(4) Municipal managers
(5) Any other elected or appointed local official
e. Constitutional Officers (Sheriff, Property Appraiser, Supervisor of Elections, Tax Collector)
f. Other
(1) Foreign university representatives
(2) Ambassadors or representatives of foreign governments
(3) Presidents or Heads of large corporations or organizations
(4) Religious or spiritual leaders - such as a Bishop or the Dalai Lama
(5) Individuals and/or groups who could attract opposition or spark controversy
(6) High-ranking members of royalty
g. Public Persons such as celebrities or public lecturers. Such individuals shall be defined as individuals who have a prominent profile in the media or general public (e.g., media presence, require speaking fees, maintain a public following, or have prior institutional invitations) Questions regarding whether an individual is a Public Person must be directed to the Vice President,
Government Relations and Economic Development.
2. Event Coordination Team (ECT): Convened and chaired by the Vice President, Government Relations and Economic Development, or their designee, for a particular event. The ECT will consist of the following
of university representatives including; but not limited to, University Communications, University Police, Sponsoring Unit, and Facilities
Management
. The chair of the ECT for a particular event has the authority to incorporate any other university representatives as appropriate.
3. Sponsoring Unit: The division, college, department, or office that is responsible for hosting the dignitary.
RESPONSIBILITIES:
1. The President:
(a) May invite any dignitary to FAU and reserves the right to preclude using university resources to host a dignitary’s visit to campus.
(b) May host dignitary visits and events, as needed.
(c) May provide welcome remarks and introduce the dignitary at any program.
(d) May present any honors bestowed upon the dignitary.
(e) May present the official university gift.
(f) May delegate any of his or her responsibilities to another university official to host a dignitary.
2. Vice President, Government Relations and Economic Development:
(a) Responsible for convening and chairing the ECT that will coordinate logistics and protocol for any dignitary event. This responsibility may be delegated to another member of the Division of Government Relations and Economic Development.
(b) Determines whether a Public Person is a dignitary for the purposes of this policy. This responsibility may be delegated to another member of the Division of Government Relations and Economic Development.
(c) Responsible for routing Visiting Dignitary Forms to the Office of the President. This responsibility may be delegated to another member of the Division of Government Relations and Economic Development.
(d) Responsible for coordinating the visits of all elected and appointed officials, as listed in Paragraphs 1(a), 1(b), and 1(d) under Definitions.] This responsibility may be delegated at the discretion of the Vice President.
3. Vice President, Student Affairs:
(a) Delegated authority to approve hosting dignitaries invited by recognized student organizations and student-run programming. In such instances, notice of the dignitary’s visit must be provided to Vice President, Government Relations and Economic Development. Notification can be provided through completion of the Notification of Visiting Dignitaries Form.
(b) In instances where a dignitary invitation is staff-led rather than student-led, the Vice President of Student Affairs will complete a Visiting Dignitary Request form and follow the appropriate process for requesting approval of an invitation prior to extending one.
4. Vice President and Director of Athletics:
(a)
Delegated authority to approve hosting dignitaries for Athletics-sponsored events and programming. In such instances, notice of the dignitary’s visit must be provided to the Vice President, Government Relations and Economic Development. Notification can be provided through completion of the Notification of Visiting Dignitaries Form.
5.
Executive Director, Osher Lifelong Learning Institute (OLLI):
(a) Delegated authority to approve hosting dignitaries for OLLI-sponsored events and programming, in recognition of the Executive Director’s reporting line to the Vice President for Government Relations and Economic Development and the advisory role of OLLI’s governing boards in shaping lecturer and guest engagement. In such instances, notice of the dignitary’s visit must be provided to the Vice President, Government Relations and Economic Development. Notification can be provided through completion of the Notification of Visiting Dignitaries Form.

.

PROCEDURES:
A. Inviting Dignitaries
It is important from a whole-of-university perspective that a strategic, coordinated, and systematic approach is taken to the selection and invitation of dignitaries to visit FAU or participate in unique FAU events. A strategic approach will ensure the appropriateness of invitations extended and will present FAU as mindful of the demands placed on dignitaries' time. It will also help to secure participation by dignitaries in university business and events, thereby ensuring a greater rate of success for faculties and units across the university.
Invitations to a dignitary may only be made directly or via an agent of the following university officials: President; Chief of Staff; Vice President for Government Relations and Economic Development; all other members of the ELT.
No other university official other than the  President, Chief of Staff, or Vice President of Government Relations and Economic Development may invite dignitaries including but not limited to the Governor of the State of Florida, presiding officers of the Florida Legislature and Congress, sitting or former President of the United States, representatives of foreign governments, and members of the Florida Board of Governors.
If a member of the university wishes to invite a dignitary to campus, the request should first be submitted to their direct supervisor. Upon approval, the supervisor will elevate the request to the appropriate Dean or Department Head, and then to the ELT member overseeing the division for final consideration.
Once this review process is complete, the Visiting Dignitaries Form must be completed by the ELT member or their appropriate designee and submitted to the Office of Government Relations and Economic Development. Written approval to invite the Dignitary must be issued to the sponsoring unit prior to extending the invitation.
B. Receiving a Request to Visit from a Dignitary
In the event a dignitary or their representative contacts a university employee, department, unit, organization, or affiliated entity expressing interest in visiting FAU, the employee or unit must first use their established organizational structure to ensure the request is reviewed and approved by the appropriate supervisor and elevated to the applicable Dean, Vice President, or Executive Director for final divisional approval.
Once divisional approval has been obtained, the sponsoring unit must complete and submit a Visiting Dignitary Request Form to the Office of Government Relations and Economic Development. No commitments, confirmations, scheduling assurances, or public announcements may be made until the request has been reviewed and approved in accordance with this policy.
C. Event Expenses
Before an FAU department or unit considers sponsoring a dignitary visit to campus, it is important to recognize that such visits—at any level—may involve significant expenses, including but not limited to security, speaker fees, and food and beverage costs. All associated expenses are the responsibility of the sponsoring department or unit. Prior to submitting a Visiting Dignitary Request Form, the requesting unit must confirm that adequate funding is available to support the event.
Refer to the posted guidance on fund allowability, procurement and credit card usage information to ensure financial compliance with statutory and regulatory requirements.
D. Timing
Visiting Dignitary Request Forms must be submitted to the Office of Government Relations and Economic Development prior to any outreach, exploratory conversations, availability inquiries, tentative scheduling holds, or engagement with the dignitary or their representatives, including agents or staff. This requirement ensures appropriate institutional vetting, coordination, and alignment with university priorities. Additionally, this process preserves the President’s discretion to extend an initial invitation when deemed appropriate.
Units are also responsible for incorporating applicable university procurement policies and procedures into the timing of their submissions to ensure planning and execution timelines align with established procurement requirements and so that the submitting unit’s internal plans for hosting the dignitary on campus are not delayed.
In most instances, Visiting Dignitary Request Forms should be submitted no fewer than thirty (30) days prior to the proposed event date and, when possible, at least ninety (90) days in advance. While exceptions to this timeline may be necessary in limited circumstances, such exceptions should be infrequent and not become standard practice. The Vice President, Government Relations and Economic Development is authorized to grant exceptions to the timeline for submission of Visiting Dignitary Request Forms.
